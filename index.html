How I work

I prefer making things on my own or working with a very small group of like-minded people, untethered from those usual trappings of business. Meetings. Estimates. Formal processes. Org charts. Reviews. It's time you never get back, better spent on the product.

I like to design directly in code. I view the frontend, backend, databases and all the languages, tech, and tooling behind a product as one canvas, rather than a stack that requires you to choose what you want to specialize in. I often switch between these layers during a day of work to avoid the natural fatigue that comes from staring at the same code for too long.

I use as few tools as possible and try to build my own, specific to what I'm working on. I'm an advocate for writing code generators to generate the tedious, yet trivial, parts of a codebase (Documentation, ORMs, CMS, API integration layers, etc.)

I prefer concreteness over abstraction in code. I avoid leaning too heavily on interfaces, abstract classes, generics, and similar concepts. I find the mental “cost-of-carry” outweighs the rarely-manifested promises of reuse, swappability, and elegance almost all the time.

When a product is on its feet, I find planning, wireframing, and deciding all of the little details of a new feature up-front are a waste of time. I prefer a few back and forths with the people I'm working with on a DoneDone ticket, then getting right to building. At that point, I have enough to work on and a good general sense of how the feature should look and feel. The rest (including unanticipated obstacles that wouldn't have been caught with more planning) are figured out in-flight.

I believe programming is as much like writing or making music as it is engineering. You can do it your own curious way and create something just as valuable as someone who goes about it differently. One example: I've never practiced TDD or write unit tests. I find integration testing more valuable, and believe other “practices” (good naming, proper encapsulation, having less people churn on a product) have the largest impact on ensuring safe code.

I was born and raised in and around Chicago and currently live in San Francisco. When not working on the things described on this page, I spend most of my time with my wife and two kids.
